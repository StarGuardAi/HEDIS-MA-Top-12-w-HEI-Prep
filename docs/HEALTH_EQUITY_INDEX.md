## ⚖️ Overview

**Health Equity Index (HEI)** is a **2027 CMS requirement** measuring health equity performance across all HEDIS measures by race, ethnicity, and other factors.

**This implementation provides:**
- **2-year head start** on 2027 compliance
- **Systematic disparity analysis** across all 12 measures
- **Competitive advantage** demonstrating commitment to health equity
- **Star rating impact** through HEI compliance

---

## 📊 HEI Components

### Disparity Measurement

**Factors Analyzed:**
- **Race**: White, Black, Hispanic, Asian, Native American, Other
- **Ethnicity**: Hispanic vs Non-Hispanic
- **Socioeconomic Status**: Income, education, insurance status
- **Geographic**: Urban vs Rural, ZIP code-level analysis
- **Language**: English vs Non-English speakers

### Measure Coverage

**All 12 HEDIS Measures Analyzed:**
- **Tier 1**: Diabetes Portfolio (5 measures)
- **Tier 2**: Cardiovascular Portfolio (4 measures)
- **Tier 3**: Cancer Screening (2 measures)
- **Tier 4**: Health Equity Index (1 measure)

### Analysis Methodology

**1. Measure-Level Disparity Analysis**

**3. Trend Analysis**

## 📈 Disparity Examples

### Example 1: Diabetes Care Disparities

**GSD (Glycemic Status Assessment) Disparities:**
- **White Members**: 85% compliance
- **Black Members**: 72% compliance
- **Hispanic Members**: 68% compliance
- **Disparity**: 13-17% gap

**Root Causes:**
- **Access Barriers**: Limited provider access in underserved areas
- **Language Barriers**: Non-English speakers face communication challenges
- **Cultural Barriers**: Cultural beliefs affecting care engagement

**Interventions:**
- **Culturally Competent Care**: Provider training and resources
- **Language Services**: Translation and interpretation services
- **Community Outreach**: Targeted outreach to underserved communities

### Example 2: Cancer Screening Disparities

**BCS (Breast Cancer Screening) Disparities:**
- **Urban Members**: 78% compliance
- **Rural Members**: 62% compliance
- **Disparity**: 16% gap

**Root Causes:**
- **Geographic Barriers**: Limited access to screening facilities
- **Transportation**: Limited transportation options
- **Provider Availability**: Fewer providers in rural areas

**Interventions:**
- **Mobile Screening Units**: Bring screening to rural communities
- **Transportation Assistance**: Provide transportation support
- **Telehealth Options**: Remote screening consultations
